About SoulStone

SoulStone, Inc. is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization

Dedicated to providing trauma-informed, community-centered support for women, caregivers, and helping professionals.

SoulStone exists to support women and caregivers who are exhausted by systems that ask them to perform, endure, and carry more than is sustainable.

We believe burnout is not a personal failure. It is a nervous system response to prolonged stress, trauma, and responsibility without adequate support.

SoulStone was created to offer something different. Not quick fixes. Not productivity hacks. Not pressure to “push through.” But grounded, trauma-informed spaces where people can slow down, reconnect to themselves, and rebuild from a place of safety.

Our Why

Burnout is not a personal failure.

Many of the people we serve are caregivers, helping professionals, women in midlife transition, survivors of chronic stress or trauma, and people who have spent years being “the strong one.”

They are capable, insightful, and deeply caring. And they are tired. SoulStone exists because rest, regulation, and healing should not be luxuries — they should be accessible, community-centered, and practical.

What We Believe

These beliefs guide how we show up and how we work.

  • The nervous system matters. Healing cannot happen in constant urgency.
  • Burnout is information, not weakness.
  • Safety comes before growth.
  • People heal in relationship, not isolation.
  • You do not need to be fixed — you need to be supported.

We believe lasting change happens when people are met with dignity, compassion, and tools that respect how trauma actually lives in the body.

How We Help

Support that is practical, relational, and grounded in real life.

SoulStone offers trauma-informed supports that blend education, embodiment, and community. Our work includes:

  • Live cohorts focused on nervous-system regulation, identity reclamation, and sustainable change
  • Trainings and workshops for caregivers, organizations, and helping professionals
  • Grounded tools and practices people can use in real life — not just theory
  • Community-based offerings that reduce isolation and restore connection

Our approach is not clinical, but it is informed. It is not prescriptive, but it is structured. It is designed to meet people where they are — without pressure to perform or “get it right.”

Our Approach

Trauma-informed, not trauma-centered.

SoulStone’s work is trauma-informed, meaning we prioritize: safety, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and respect for lived experience.

We do not ask people to relive their trauma. We focus on present-moment regulation, capacity-building, and integration.

Healing does not require re-opening every wound. It requires conditions where the body and mind can finally exhale.

Our Ecosystem

One mission, multiple expressions of care

SoulStone, Inc. is the nonprofit home of this work. Connected to SoulStone are mission-aligned initiatives that expand how support is offered:

  • The Reclamation Co. — education, tools, and offerings focused on nervous-system reset, identity reclamation, and sustainable living
  • Appalachian Soul Provisions — tangible goods created with intention, rooted in place, care, and nourishment

Each branch serves the same mission: helping people return to themselves — gently, honestly, and without shame.

Looking Ahead

We grow with integrity, not urgency.

SoulStone is growing intentionally. As we expand, we are building: additional live cohorts, community-based programming, professional trainings, and partnerships that strengthen local and regional support systems.
